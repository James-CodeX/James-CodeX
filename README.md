### Who I am

I'm James ‚Äî a developer from Nairobi who keeps ending up at the intersection of software and the physical world.

I got into code because I wanted to understand how things *actually* work. That curiosity led me down a weird path: writing firmware, training models, building dashboards, and deploying apps ‚Äî sometimes all for the same project.

My thing is building software that solves problems people in my corner of the world actually face. Not another SaaS clone. Not another todo app. Things like: *can a sensor tell a beekeeper their queen has died before they lose the colony?*

Turns out, yes. That's what I'm here for.

### What I'm thinking about lately

Building at the edge of software and the physical world means most of my interesting problems don't have Stack Overflow answers. I spend a lot of time in documentation, in datasheets, and in the space between "the sensor says X" and "what does X actually mean."

I also think a lot about what software looks like when it's built *for* Kenya, not just *deployed* in Kenya. The infrastructure assumptions are different. The users are different. The problems are different. That's not a constraint ‚Äî it's the most interesting design space I know.
